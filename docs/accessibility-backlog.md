## Active Recommendations

### P2 - [TB-0032] Enlarge the collapsed `Privacy choices` launcher to meet WCAG 2.2 target-size expectations
- Evidence: After the banner closes, [ConsentManager.tsx](/Users/macdaddy/CodexWork/TrustedBums/trustedbums/src/components/ConsentManager.tsx:141) still renders the persistent launcher as a fixed `Button` with `h-5`, `px-2`, and `text-[10px]`. `h-5` fixes the control height at `20px`, so the target cannot contain the `24 by 24 CSS pixel` square WCAG 2.2 uses for the minimum-size requirement. A fresh local Playwright measurement on `http://127.0.0.1:8080/` after dismissing the banner returned an approximate bounding box of `53.58 by 20 CSS pixels`, which confirms the active failure is the launcher height rather than a stale source-only inference. The collapsed launcher is a site-wide legal control, not inline text and not a user-agent-sized control.
- Why it matters: The privacy launcher is the durable entry point for changing consent choices. Small fixed targets are harder to activate for touch and motor-impaired users, especially on mobile public routes.
- Recommendation: Increase the collapsed launcher hit area to at least `24 by 24 CSS pixels`, or replace it with an equally discoverable same-page control that already meets the minimum target-size expectation.
- Acceptance criteria: The collapsed `Privacy choices` control exposes at least a `24 by 24 CSS pixel` hit area on public and portal pages, remains visually discoverable after the banner closes, and does not require a separate page or reset flow to reach a compliant control.

## Current Standards And Time-Sensitive Notes

- WCAG 2.2 remains the current W3C baseline for this backlog. The current W3C WCAG overview says WCAG 2.2 was published on 2023-10-05 and updated on 2024-12-12, and W3C still encourages teams to use the latest WCAG version. Source: https://www.w3.org/WAI/standards-guidelines/wcag/
- Current WAI-ARIA APG modal dialog guidance still says dialogs need either `aria-labelledby` that points to a visible dialog title or an `aria-label`, and it strongly recommends a visible dialog-close button in the tab sequence. That remains directly applicable to the mobile sidebar sheet. Source: https://www.w3.org/WAI/ARIA/apg/patterns/dialog-modal/
- Current WCAG 2.2 target-size guidance still says pointer targets should be at least `24 by 24 CSS pixels` unless a defined exception applies. That still applies to the collapsed site-wide `Privacy choices` launcher. Source: https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/target-size-minimum.html
