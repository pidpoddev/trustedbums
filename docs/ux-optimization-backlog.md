## Active Recommendations

### P1 - Add field-level validation and reassurance to the homepage contact form
- Evidence: The public lead form in [src/pages/Index.tsx](</Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted Bums LOCAL/trustedbums/src/pages/Index.tsx>) validates only three checks in `submitContactForm` and responds with a toast saying “A little more detail helps.” The individual fields do not render inline errors, error summary links, `aria-invalid` state, or next-step reassurance such as expected response timing or privacy handling. Current guidance continues to favor putting clear error text near the field and, when relevant, in a summary so users can quickly recover ([W3C WAI Forms Tutorial](https://www.w3.org/WAI/tutorials/forms/), updated March 27, 2026; [GOV.UK Error message](https://design-system.service.gov.uk/components/error-message/); [GOV.UK Error summary](https://design-system.service.gov.uk/components/error-summary/)).
- Why it matters: This is the main public handoff for buyers and prospective Bums. Toast-only validation increases avoidable form abandonment and makes the brand feel less dependable at the exact point where trust and clarity need to be highest.
- Recommendation: Keep the toast for broad failure states, but add inline validation for each invalid field, an error summary on submit, and a short trust cue near the submit action that explains response timing and how the information will be used.
- Acceptance criteria: Invalid submit shows field-level messages beside each failing field and links to them from a summary; invalid fields expose accessible error state; success and failure states remain visible long enough to act on; the submit area includes a short expectation-setting note such as response window or privacy handling.

### P1 - Explain blocked client-role redirects on the dashboard
- Evidence: [src/components/ClientAccessRoute.tsx](</Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted Bums LOCAL/trustedbums/src/components/ClientAccessRoute.tsx>) still redirects unauthorized client users to `/client/dashboard` with `state={{ deniedFrom: location.pathname }}`, but [src/pages/client/ClientDashboard.tsx](</Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted Bums LOCAL/trustedbums/src/pages/client/ClientDashboard.tsx>) still does not read that state or render any denied-access notice. [tests/e2e/authenticated-role-smoke.spec.ts](</Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted Bums LOCAL/trustedbums/tests/e2e/authenticated-role-smoke.spec.ts>) covers the redirect path, but current QA could not reach the environment to visually verify the post-redirect state. Guidance still favors concise status messaging that tells users what happened and what to do next ([W3C WAI user notification guidance](https://www.w3.org/WAI/tutorials/forms/notifications/), [GOV.UK Error message](https://design-system.service.gov.uk/components/error-message/)).
- Why it matters: Silent redirects make role boundaries feel arbitrary, create unnecessary support traffic, and reduce confidence for client finance and client member users who are trying to understand what their role can actually do.
- Recommendation: Read `deniedFrom` on the client dashboard and show a dismissible notice naming the blocked destination, the current client access role, and the nearest allowed workflow.
- Acceptance criteria: A redirected client user sees a visible notice on the next dashboard load naming the blocked destination and current role; the notice includes a next-step action to an allowed page; the notice does not appear on ordinary dashboard visits; route-guard or visual coverage verifies the denied-state render.

### P1 - Reduce commission-plan request friction to a minimum viable schedule
- Evidence: [src/pages/client/ClientOpportunityNew.tsx](</Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted Bums LOCAL/trustedbums/src/pages/client/ClientOpportunityNew.tsx>) still marks `year_2_rate` through `year_6_plus_rate` as `required`, while the submit payload still converts blank later-year rates to `0` with `Number(requestForm.year_n_rate || 0)`. The UI therefore forces six percentages even though the submit logic already tolerates omitted later years. Current W3C forms guidance says to ask only for what is required because extra fields increase abandonment and confusion ([W3C WAI Forms Tutorial](https://www.w3.org/WAI/tutorials/forms/), updated March 27, 2026).
- Why it matters: Clients often know the near-term commission structure before they know the full long-tail payout schedule. Requiring a six-year schedule up front increases hesitation, invented data, and avoidable drop-off during registration.
- Recommendation: Make year 1 the only default required rate, move later-year rates behind an explicit multi-year schedule choice or mark them optional, and explain how omitted years will be treated in review.
- Acceptance criteria: A client can submit a commission-plan request with a minimum viable required set; later-year fields are clearly optional unless the user opts into a multi-year schedule; helper text explains fallback handling for omitted years; labels and validation distinguish required from optional inputs.

### P2 - Label note-only training entries so they do not look broken
- Evidence: [src/pages/client/ClientTrainings.tsx](</Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted Bums LOCAL/trustedbums/src/pages/client/ClientTrainings.tsx>) still supports a `REFERENCE_ONLY` filter for entries without `resource_url`, but cards without links or attachments still render with the same document framing and no visible badge, explanatory copy, or alternate CTA state. The only signal is the absence of actions.
- Why it matters: Users scanning the library cannot tell at a glance whether an item is intentionally a short briefing note or whether the asset is incomplete. That ambiguity slows triage and weakens trust in the content library.
- Recommendation: Add a visible “Reference note” treatment for entries without links or attachments and mirror that terminology in filters, empty states, and preview text.
- Acceptance criteria: Every note-only training card displays a visible label and short explanatory copy; note-only entries are distinguishable from broken or missing assets at scan speed; filter terminology matches the card treatment; visual coverage includes at least one note-only card state.
