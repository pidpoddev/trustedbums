## Active Recommendations

### Superseded - Preserve typed company names in the public signup flow
- Evidence: `src/components/SignupIntentDialog.tsx` still calls `setCompanyName("")` when the selected role is `CLIENT` and `getKnownClientForEmail(email)` returns no match, so editing the email can erase a manually entered company name before signup is complete. The latest public signup screenshot in GitHub `Visual UI Audit` artifact `27083467531` (`mobile-chrome-public-signup-intent.png`) confirms that this company-name field remains part of the core signup modal. Baymard still flags clearing entered form data as a high-friction pattern: https://baymard.com/learn/checkout-flow-ux-optimization
- Why it matters: This is an early trust moment for prospective clients. Silent data loss makes the intake flow feel brittle before the visitor has shared enough information to justify the friction.
- Recommendation: Preserve manually entered `companyName` unless the user explicitly accepts a matched workspace or switches away from the client path. Keep workspace-match guidance, but separate existing-workspace confirmation from first-time workspace requests.
- Acceptance criteria: Editing the email no longer clears a manually entered company name when no workspace match exists; matched workspaces still prefill clearly; first-time workspace requests retain the typed company name unless the user intentionally replaces it.

### Superseded - Replace toast-only contact-form recovery with inline errors and reassurance
- Evidence: `src/pages/Index.tsx` still validates only `name`, `email`, and `message` inside `submitContactForm` and responds with destructive toasts, while the form markup still lacks inline errors, linked summary messaging, and `aria-invalid`/`aria-describedby` wiring. The latest public-home mobile screenshot in GitHub `Visual UI Audit` artifact `27083467531` (`mobile-chrome-public-home.png`) shows the contact area ending in a single CTA without an inline recovery pattern or expectation-setting message near submit. Current W3C and GOV.UK guidance still recommends field-level errors plus a linked summary for invalid submit states: https://design-system.w3.org/styles/form-errors.html and https://design-system.service.gov.uk/components/error-summary/
- Why it matters: This is the main public handoff for buyers and future Bums. Toast-only validation makes users guess which field failed and weakens trust exactly when they are deciding whether to share contact details.
- Recommendation: Add field-level validation messages, a linked error summary on submit, `aria-invalid` and `aria-describedby` wiring, and a short reassurance note near submit that explains reply timing or handling.
- Acceptance criteria: Invalid submit shows inline errors and a linked summary; invalid fields expose accessible error state; users can recover without guessing; the submit area includes a short reassurance message about follow-up timing or handling.

### P2 - Reduce the first-visit consent banner footprint on mobile
- Evidence: A fresh QA-backed local mobile screenshot from `http://127.0.0.1:8080` (`/private/tmp/trustedbums-ux-20260608/public-home-mobile-qaenv.png`) shows the `Privacy choices` panel covering most of the first viewport and sitting directly over the hero copy and first CTA. `src/components/ConsentManager.tsx` currently opens that panel by default when no consent record exists and renders a first-layer sheet with `max-h-[78vh]`, multi-paragraph copy, and stacked actions on small screens. Current ICO consent guidance shows a mobile first layer with equally prominent `accept`, `reject`, and `customise` options while pushing detailed controls into the second layer: https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/direct-marketing-and-privacy-and-electronic-communications/guidance-on-the-use-of-storage-and-access-technologies/how-do-we-manage-consent-in-practice/
- Why it matters: First-time mobile visitors cannot quickly read the value proposition or reach the opening CTA, so the consent mechanism becomes the dominant experience instead of a short compliance step.
- Recommendation: Keep equal-prominence consent choices, but shorten the first-layer copy and height on small screens so the hero headline and first CTA remain visible. Push detailed category explanations and toggle-heavy controls into the existing `Customize` layer, and coordinate the change with Trust/Legal so the consent posture stays compliant.
- Acceptance criteria: On first visit at mobile widths, the banner still presents equally prominent consent choices, but the hero headline and at least one primary CTA remain visible without dismissing the banner; longer explanatory detail lives behind `Customize`; Trust/Legal review is recorded for the consent-copy/layout change.

### Superseded - Make client access and agreement blockers explicit
- Evidence: `src/components/ClientAccessRoute.tsx` still redirects blocked client users to `/client/dashboard` with `state={{ deniedFrom: location.pathname }}`, but `src/pages/client/ClientDashboard.tsx` still does not read `deniedFrom` or explain the redirect. The dashboard’s finance and non-finance next-action cards still route agreement recovery to `/client/profile`, even though the dedicated agreement workspace lives at `src/pages/client/ClientAgreements.tsx` and the action page lives at `src/pages/client/ClientTerms.tsx`. `src/pages/client/ClientTerms.tsx` still labels the deferral action `Skip This Login`, which obscures the session-scoped nature of the deferral. W3C guidance still recommends explaining blocked states and next steps clearly: https://www.w3.org/WAI/tutorials/forms/notifications/
- Why it matters: Silent redirects, misrouted agreement recovery, and ambiguous deferral language make role boundaries and legal requirements feel arbitrary. That increases support load and lowers confidence in the client portal.
- Recommendation: Surface a dismissible dashboard notice when `deniedFrom` is present, name the current client role, explain the blocked destination, and route agreement actions to `/client/agreements` or `/client/terms` instead of `Company Profile`. Replace `Skip This Login` with session-scoped wording such as `Review later this session`, and require Legal/Compliance owner review before shipping agreement-deferral copy changes.
- Acceptance criteria: A redirected user sees a visible notice naming the blocked page, current role, and next allowed action; dashboard agreement-recovery CTAs route to the agreement workspace; deferral copy makes the session scope explicit; the implementation records Legal/Compliance owner review for the agreement-copy change.

### P2 - Surface urgency and next-step cues in admin handoff queues
- Evidence: `src/pages/admin/AdminHandoffs.tsx` still sorts target responses, intro requests, and public contact conversions by age, but the tables still expose only target/contact, status, owner, created time, update, and actions. The underlying data model already includes `admin_next_action`, `admin_priority`, and `notification_error` in `src/lib/contactApi.ts`, and notification failure is still surfaced separately in `src/pages/admin/AdminTroubleshooting.tsx`. Current USWDS guidance still recommends improving dense-table scannability by reducing inference burden and surfacing the most important signals directly: https://designsystem.digital.gov/components/table/
- Why it matters: This is the admin rescue queue for leads, target responses, and intro requests. Without urgency and next-step context, admins have to infer what is actually stuck and what to do next.
- Recommendation: Surface priority, next action, stale or SLA state, and notification failure directly in each queue row or responsive summary so admins can triage without leaving the page.
- Acceptance criteria: Open items older than the chosen SLA threshold are visually distinct; each row shows the next required action; contact submissions with failed notifications are visible in the handoff queue; admins can distinguish fresh, stale, and unowned work at a glance.
