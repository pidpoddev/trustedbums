- Why it matters: If this worktree ships as-is, a legitimate opportunity-managing role hits a dead-end at the exact point where commission-plan guidance is supposed to unblock the workflow. Do not link Members to a route they cannot open.

### P1 - [TB-0064] Make the client-only signup CTA labels explicit across `/`
- Evidence: The client-only path on `/` is still labeled three different ways in exact-head source: the header button remains `Sign up` in [Index.tsx](/Users/macdaddy/CodexWork/TrustedBums/trustedbums/src/pages/Index.tsx:286), the hero CTA says `Create Client account` at [Index.tsx](/Users/macdaddy/CodexWork/TrustedBums/trustedbums/src/pages/Index.tsx:354), and the contact-section CTA still uses `Client signup` at [Index.tsx](/Users/macdaddy/CodexWork/TrustedBums/trustedbums/src/pages/Index.tsx:587). Fresh local public smoke `4/4` and the local mobile public visual audit both passed on the `qa`-mode `127.0.0.1:8080` preview, so this remains wording drift rather than a broken dialog or broken signup handoff. Current W3C and GOV.UK guidance still favors labels that clearly describe the action users are about to take: [W3C labels guidance](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/headings-and-labels.html) and [GOV.UK button guidance](https://design-system.service.gov.uk/components/button/).
- Why it matters: After the landing split, the generic header `Sign up` label still reads like a universal path even though `/` is client-only and `/bums` owns Bum applications. Mixed client CTA labels add hesitation right where a buyer or operator is deciding whether they are in the correct funnel.
- Recommendation: Standardize the client-only signup action on `/` to one explicit client label system such as `Create Client account`, and reserve Bum-application language for `/bums` and Bum-specific CTAs. If the header needs a shorter mobile label, add nearby context that still makes the client-only action unmistakable.
- Acceptance criteria: Every CTA on `/` that opens the client-locked signup dialog uses explicit client wording; the public header no longer uses generic `Sign up` for that path; Bum application CTAs remain Bum-specific; and smoke plus visual tests are updated to the approved label set.

### P2 - [TB-0065] Reduce the first-layer consent banner footprint on mobile
- Evidence: A fresh Pixel 7 local capture on the `qa`-mode `127.0.0.1:8080/?consent=reset` preview still shows the first consent layer pushing the hero's primary intro CTA below the fold on first visit even though the headline remains visible. Current source still renders that first layer with `max-h-[78vh]`, two text blocks, and a stacked action group in [ConsentManager.tsx](/Users/macdaddy/CodexWork/TrustedBums/trustedbums/src/components/ConsentManager.tsx:93). The privacy flow itself remains healthy in exact-head local smoke and the local public visual audit passed on 2026-06-15; the issue is presentation weight, not missing controls. Current ICO guidance still emphasizes equally accessible choice controls and warns that desktop-sized message boxes can become hard to read or interact with on mobile: [ICO consent-in-practice guidance](https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/direct-marketing-and-privacy-and-electronic-communications/guidance-on-the-use-of-storage-and-access-technologies/how-do-we-manage-consent-in-practice/) and [W3C reflow guidance](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/reflow.html).
- Why it matters: The consent step still interrupts the first mobile conversion moment by pushing the page's main client CTA out of the initial view, turning a compliance step into the dominant product interaction.
- Recommendation: Keep equally prominent consent choices, but tighten the first-layer copy and action layout so the hero headline and the primary client CTA remain visible on first mobile visit. Keep the granular category explanations and toggles behind `Customize`, and record Trust or Legal review for any copy or layout changes.
- Acceptance criteria: On first visit at mobile widths, the initial consent layer preserves equally prominent choice controls while keeping the hero headline and the page's main client CTA visible without scrolling; detailed category explanations stay behind `Customize`; and the revised presentation is reviewed by the Trust or Legal owner.
