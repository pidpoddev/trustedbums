If the header needs a shorter mobile label, add nearby context that still makes the client-only action unmistakable.

- Evidence: A fresh Pixel 7 local capture on the qa-mode `127.0.0.1:8080/?consent=reset` preview still shows the first consent layer pushing the hero's primary client CTA below the fold on first visit even though the headline remains visible. Current source still renders that first layer with `max-h-[78vh]`, two text blocks, and a stacked action group in [ConsentManager.tsx](/Users/macdaddy/CodexWork/TrustedBums/trustedbums/src/components/ConsentManager.tsx:94), [ConsentManager.tsx](/Users/macdaddy/CodexWork/TrustedBums/trustedbums/src/components/ConsentManager.tsx:130), and [ConsentManager.tsx](/Users/macdaddy/CodexWork/TrustedBums/trustedbums/src/components/ConsentManager.tsx:131). The privacy flow itself remains healthy in exact-head local smoke and the local public visual audit passed on 2026-06-17; the issue is presentation weight, not missing controls. Current ICO guidance still says consent information and choice mechanisms should be as accessible as possible, warns that desktop-sized message boxes can be hard to read or interact with on mobile, and expects refusal to be as easy as acceptance: [ICO consent in practice guidance](https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/direct-marketing-and-privacy-and-electronic-communications/guidance-on-the-use-of-storage-and-access-technologies/how-do-we-manage-consent-in-practice/) and [W3C reflow guidance](https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG22/#reflow).
- Why it matters: The consent step still interrupts the first mobile conversion moment by pushing the page's main client CTA out of the initial view, turning a compliance step into the dominant product interaction.
- Recommendation: Keep equally prominent consent choices, but tighten the first-layer copy and action layout so the hero headline and the primary client CTA remain visible on first mobile visit. Keep the granular category explanations and toggles behind `Customize`, and record Trust or Legal review for any copy or layout changes.
- Acceptance criteria: On first visit at mobile widths, the initial consent layer preserves equally prominent choice controls while keeping the hero headline and the page's main client CTA visible without scrolling; detailed category explanations stay behind `Customize`; and the revised presentation is reviewed by the Trust or Legal owner.
