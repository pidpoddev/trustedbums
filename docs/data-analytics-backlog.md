## Active Recommendations

### P1 - Add an explicit admin-email reporting access rule
- Evidence: `src/pages/admin/AdminEmails.tsx` and `src/pages/admin/AdminReports.tsx` load raw campaign, delivery, and engagement data through `listAdminEmailDeliveries()`, `listAdminEmailCampaigns()`, and `listAdminEmailEngagementSummary()`. Live `pg_policies` inspection shows `admin_email_campaigns`, `admin_email_deliveries`, and `admin_email_events` are currently admin-only in RLS, but `docs/business-access-rules.md` still has no business rule covering admin email templates, deliveries, campaign history, recipient metadata, or engagement reporting.
- Why it matters: Raw recipient emails, message bodies, suppression state, and engagement rows are sensitive operational data. Right now the restriction lives in source and RLS, but not in the business access contract that Security, QA, Data, and Lead Developer are supposed to validate against.
- Recommendation: Add an `Admin email operations and reporting` rule to `docs/business-access-rules.md` that explicitly keeps template management, campaign history, recipient-level deliveries, and engagement rows admin-only. If Trusted Bums later wants non-admin reporting, expose aggregate-safe campaign metrics through a separate surface that omits recipient identity, message body, and suppression data.
- Acceptance criteria: `docs/business-access-rules.md` names allowed admin uses, denied client/Bum/public access, and any future aggregate-only exception path; route/API allow-deny coverage proves non-admin roles cannot read raw delivery or campaign data; any future shared metrics surface is aggregate-only.

### P2 - Make admin email analytics aggregate-first before volume grows
- Evidence: `supabase/functions/send-admin-email/index.ts` still caps `list_deliveries`, `list_engagement`, and `list_campaigns` at `50` rows each. `src/pages/admin/AdminEmails.tsx` builds its `Tracked engagement` card directly from `opened_at` and `clicked_at` on the currently loaded delivery sample. Live Supabase counts are still small today (`27` deliveries, `16` campaigns, `20` events), but the live Performance Advisor already flags unindexed foreign keys across the `admin_email_*` tables. Current Apple guidance for Mail Privacy Protection says senders cannot rely on seeing whether a recipient opened an email.
- Why it matters: The current admin email dashboard will silently become sample-based once volume passes the hard cap, and the headline engagement card overstates the trustworthiness of open metrics even before scale becomes a problem. That weakens campaign decision support for operations.
- Recommendation: Add date-window filters and pagination or cursor support for admin email lists, add a summary RPC/view for campaign-level counts that reads the full filtered dataset, and add the missing supporting indexes for the admin-email foreign keys the Supabase advisor is flagging. Relabel opens as a partial or proxy signal and prioritize delivered, failed, clicked, replied, or conversion-linked metrics for decision-making.
- Acceptance criteria: Admin email list operations accept a date window and pagination/cursor inputs; summary cards read aggregate counts rather than whichever 50 rows happened to load; the prioritized `admin_email_*` advisor warnings are cleared or explicitly waived with reason; UI copy no longer presents opens as a decision-grade engagement metric.

### P2 - Trim terms-acceptance analytics payloads to metadata-only fields
- Evidence: `src/lib/portalApi.ts` `listTermsAcceptances()` selects `terms_versions(id, version, title, body, faq_body, change_summary, audience, is_custom, custom_label, created_at)`, and the same broad query feeds `src/pages/admin/AdminDashboard.tsx`, `src/pages/admin/AdminClients.tsx`, `src/pages/admin/AdminBums.tsx`, and `src/pages/admin/AdminReports.tsx`. Live SQL shows `19` `terms_acceptances` rows across `5` `terms_versions`, with a maximum `body` length of `5,970` characters and maximum `faq_body` length of `2,464` characters.
- Why it matters: Admin analytics pages are loading full legal-document content when they only need acceptance history, version labels, and timing metadata. That increases payload size, mixes document authoring content into reporting queries, and makes later reporting access changes harder to reason about.
- Recommendation: Split acceptance reporting from terms authoring by creating a lighter metadata select or view for analytics pages. Keep full `body` and `faq_body` loading only on dedicated terms detail or editor flows that actually need document text.
- Acceptance criteria: Acceptance-reporting queries stop selecting `body` and `faq_body`; admin dashboard, client admin review, and Bum admin review still show the acceptance metadata they need; full legal content remains available only in terms-management flows.

## Business Access And Reporting Rules

- Admin email operations and reporting: Admins need raw template, campaign, delivery, suppression, and engagement data to run marketplace communications and troubleshoot sends. Client Admins, Client Finance, Client Members, Bums, and Public Visitors should not see recipient-level campaign rows, raw message bodies, suppression state, or recipient engagement history. Sensitivity: high, because the data includes recipient identity, message content, and behavior signals. Add or clarify in `docs/business-access-rules.md`: an explicit admin-only rule for raw email operations plus an aggregate-only exception path if non-admin reporting is ever needed. Verification: route/API negative-role tests plus admin-only positive coverage.
- Admin email KPIs shared outside admin: If leadership or client-facing reporting ever needs communication metrics, those viewers should receive aggregate delivery, failure, click, reply, or conversion metrics only, not raw recipient rows. Sensitivity: medium to high depending on whether campaign filters imply identifiable recipients. Add or clarify in `docs/business-access-rules.md`: aggregate-safe metrics are allowed only when recipient identity, bodies, and suppression details are removed. Verification: response-shape assertions and column-level export checks.
- Terms-acceptance reporting: Source-backed inference from current policy and admin usage: Admins need cross-company acceptance history; company-scoped non-admin users may need only their own company acceptance status metadata, not full legal-document bodies inside reporting queries. Sensitivity: legal-history metadata plus document content. Add or clarify in `docs/business-access-rules.md`: separate acceptance metadata access from full legal-document authoring content. Verification: reporting queries return metadata-only fields, and dedicated terms-management flows remain the only readers of full document text.
