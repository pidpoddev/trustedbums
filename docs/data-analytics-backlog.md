## Active Recommendations

### P1 - [TB-0044] Split Client Finance reporting into a finance-safe read model
- Evidence: Exact head `d360570` still lets every finance-capable viewer hit `listCustomerPaymentReports()` and `listClaimInvoices()` with joins that include `opportunity_claims(contact_name, contact_company, bum_user_id)` and reporter `profiles(full_name, email)` in `src/lib/portalApi.ts`. Those shared reads still back `src/pages/client/ClientDashboard.tsx`, `src/pages/client/ClientPayments.tsx`, `src/pages/client/ClientReports.tsx`, and `src/pages/client/ClientExports.tsx` for `CLIENT_FINANCE`. Live production volume is currently `0` `customer_payment_reports` rows and `0` `claim_invoices` rows, so the least-privilege gap remains source-backed rather than exercised by richer live finance data.
- Why it matters: The visible Client Finance cards and CSVs are finance-safer than earlier versions, but the browser payload still hydrates operational contact, Bum, and reporter data that finance users do not need. That keeps least-privilege enforcement in app code instead of in the read model and leaves future finance exports harder to validate.
- Recommendation: Create a dedicated finance-safe projection for Client Finance payment and invoice reads, either through narrower role-aware selects or a dedicated view/RPC, and route Client Finance dashboard, report, export, and payment surfaces through that projection. Commit the matching finance reporting rule text in `docs/business-access-rules.md` instead of leaving it only in the current dirty worktree.
- Acceptance criteria: Client Finance payloads omit operational relationship fields such as contact names, contact companies, Bum IDs, and unrelated reporter identity; Client Admin and Admin flows retain richer fields only where required; exact-head business-access docs describe the finance-safe read model; and positive/negative response-shape tests cover both roles.

### P1 - [TB-0045] Add an explicit admin-email reporting access rule
- Evidence: Exact head `d360570` still has no `Admin Email Operations And Reporting` section in `docs/business-access-rules.md`. The current dirty worktree already contains draft finance and admin-email reporting rules, but those additions are not committed and therefore are not exact-head evidence. Live schema and view shape still expose recipient-level fields such as `recipient_email`, `full_name`, `company_name`, `subject_snapshot`, `body_snapshot`, `clicked_url`, `user_agent`, and `ip_hash`.
- Why it matters: The product already treats this as admin tooling, but the committed business contract that Security, QA, Data, and Lead Developer are supposed to validate before future sharing changes is still missing. Leaving the rule only in a dirty docs diff keeps the tracker and future specialist runs out of sync with shipped truth.
- Recommendation: Commit an `Admin Email Operations And Reporting` rule to `docs/business-access-rules.md` that keeps templates, campaign history, recipient-level deliveries, suppressions, engagement rows, and content snapshots admin-only by default. If leadership reporting is needed later, require a separate aggregate-only surface that strips recipient identity, message body snapshots, click URLs, and suppression details.
- Acceptance criteria: Exact-head `docs/business-access-rules.md` names the allowed admin use, denied client and Bum access, sensitive fields, and any future aggregate-only exception path; route/API coverage proves non-admin roles cannot read raw admin-email reporting rows; and future shared KPI work references the aggregate-only exception instead of the raw tables.

### P2 - [TB-0046] Make admin email metrics aggregate-first and stop treating opens as decision-grade
- Evidence: Exact head `d360570` still caps `list_deliveries`, `list_engagement`, and `list_campaigns` at `50` rows each inside `supabase/functions/send-admin-email/index.ts`, and `src/pages/admin/AdminEmails.tsx` still derives the headline `Tracked engagement` card from `deliveries.filter((delivery) => delivery.opened_at)` and `deliveries.filter((delivery) => delivery.clicked_at)`. Live production counts on 2026-06-12 still show `29` deliveries, `18` campaigns, `24` events, `23` `OPEN` events, and `1` `CLICK` event. The current Supabase performance advisor also flags unindexed admin-email foreign keys, including `admin_email_campaigns.template_id`, `admin_email_campaigns.created_by`, `admin_email_deliveries.campaign_id`, `admin_email_deliveries.template_id`, `admin_email_deliveries.recipient_profile_id`, `admin_email_deliveries.created_by`, `admin_email_events.delivery_id`, and `admin_email_events.recipient_profile_id`.
- Why it matters: The dashboard becomes sample-based once activity exceeds the list cap, and its most prominent engagement number already leans on the least trustworthy signal in the dataset. The live advisor warnings confirm the query-shape debt is real, not just theoretical.
- Recommendation: Add aggregate summary helpers for admin email campaign and engagement KPIs, move the headline cards to those aggregates, add bounded filters or pagination for delivery and campaign tables, relabel opens as partial or proxy signals, and add or explicitly waive the admin-email foreign-key indexes that the live advisor flagged once the final query shape is set.
- Acceptance criteria: Admin email summary cards read aggregate data instead of capped lists; deliveries and campaigns support bounded filters or pagination; open metrics are labeled as proxy signals; and the next SQL-backed review either confirms the flagged `admin_email_*` indexes exist or records an explicit waiver.
