# Trusted Bums Data Analytics Backlog

_Last updated: 2026-06-08 by Codex daily data analytics engineer automation._

## Executive Read

Current source, test, and live Supabase evidence narrow the analytics backlog to three active governance and reporting gaps. Business-date finance reporting remains fixed across Client, Admin, and Bum views, Client Finance export cards remain finance-safe, admin performance telemetry now reads server-side summary helpers instead of browser-side percentile math, and the earlier local extension-env blocker is no longer current: raw shell env is still empty, but sourcing `.env.qa` restores the expected contract and `qa:target-preflight` now passes against `https://trustedbums.com`.

The remaining work is narrower and more specific. Client Finance pages still fetch richer operational finance payloads than the business rules allow, admin email reporting still lacks an explicit business access rule even though the live tables are admin-only in RLS, and admin email KPIs still depend on capped list endpoints plus open-tracking signals that Apple explicitly makes unreliable.

## Active Recommendations

### P1 - [TB-0044] Split Client Finance reporting into a finance-safe read model
- Evidence: `src/lib/portalApi.ts` `listCustomerPaymentReports()` selects `opportunity_claims(id, contact_name, contact_company, bum_user_id)` plus reporter `profiles(id, full_name, email)` for every finance-capable viewer, and `listClaimInvoices()` does the same. Those functions are called from `src/pages/client/ClientDashboard.tsx`, `src/pages/client/ClientPayments.tsx`, `src/pages/client/ClientReports.tsx`, and `src/pages/client/ClientExports.tsx` for Client Finance users. `docs/business-access-rules.md` already says Client Finance should receive only finance-safe Opportunity context, and `src/test/clientExportsAccess.test.ts` proves the CSV cards are narrowed but does not prove the underlying browser payload is.
- Why it matters: The visible Client Finance exports are now safer, but the browser query path can still hydrate operational relationship details that finance users do not need, including contact names, contact companies, Bum IDs, and reporter identity. That weakens least-privilege enforcement and makes future reporting or export hardening harder to verify.
- Recommendation: Create a finance-safe projection for Client Finance payment and invoice reads, either through a dedicated view/RPC or narrower role-aware selects, and route Client Finance dashboard/report/export/payment pages through that projection. Keep the richer operational/admin payload only for Admin or Client Admin flows that actually need it. Flag the change for Security and QA because it touches business-access boundaries.
- Acceptance criteria: Client Finance payment and invoice responses omit operational relationship fields such as contact names, contact companies, Bum IDs, unrelated reporter identity, and any other non-finance fields; Client Admin and Admin paths retain the richer payload only where needed; automated tests cover both positive finance access and response-shape denials for the removed fields.

### P1 - [TB-0045] Add an explicit admin-email reporting access rule
- Evidence: `src/pages/admin/AdminEmails.tsx` and `src/pages/admin/AdminReports.tsx` load campaign, delivery, and engagement data through `listAdminEmailDeliveries()`, `listAdminEmailCampaigns()`, and `listAdminEmailEngagementSummary()`. Live Supabase `pg_policies` inspection shows `admin_email_campaigns`, `admin_email_deliveries`, and `admin_email_events` are currently admin-only through `private.is_admin()`, but `docs/business-access-rules.md` still has no rule covering admin email templates, campaigns, recipient-level deliveries, suppression state, or engagement reporting.
- Why it matters: Raw admin email reporting includes recipient identity, message content snapshots, suppression state, and behavior signals. The enforcement exists in live schema and edge-function code today, but not in the business contract Security, QA, Data, and Lead Developer are supposed to validate before future access or reporting changes land.
- Recommendation: Add an `Admin email operations and reporting` rule to `docs/business-access-rules.md` that keeps templates, campaign history, recipient-level deliveries, suppression state, and engagement rows admin-only by default. If Trusted Bums later wants leadership or client-visible email metrics, require a separate aggregate-only reporting surface that strips recipient identity, message body snapshots, and suppression details. Flag the rule update for Security and Lead Developer review before any sharing exception is implemented.
- Acceptance criteria: `docs/business-access-rules.md` names the allowed admin use, denied client/Bum/public access, sensitive fields, and any future aggregate-only exception path; route/API allow-deny coverage proves non-admin roles cannot read raw campaign or delivery rows; any future shared KPI surface is explicitly aggregate-only.

### P2 - [TB-0046] Make admin email metrics aggregate-first and stop treating opens as decision-grade
- Evidence: `supabase/functions/send-admin-email/index.ts` caps `list_deliveries`, `list_engagement`, and `list_campaigns` at `50` rows each. `src/pages/admin/AdminEmails.tsx` computes the headline `Tracked engagement` card from `deliveries.filter((delivery) => delivery.opened_at)` and `deliveries.filter((delivery) => delivery.clicked_at)`, so the KPI is derived from whichever rows were loaded rather than an aggregate over the full filtered dataset. Live Supabase counts are still small today (`16` campaigns, `27` deliveries, `20` events), but direct catalog inspection also shows missing leading indexes on multiple `admin_email_*` foreign keys. Apple’s current Mail privacy guidance says Protect Mail Activity privately downloads remote content and prevents senders from learning when and how many times a message is viewed, which makes open tracking a partial signal rather than a decision-grade metric. Supabase’s current advisor guidance still recommends indexing foreign-key columns used in joins.
- Why it matters: The current admin email dashboard will silently become sample-based once row counts exceed the hard cap, and its headline engagement signal overstates how trustworthy open data is even before scale becomes a problem. That weakens operational decisions around campaigns, automations, and outreach quality.
- Recommendation: Add aggregate summary helpers for admin email campaigns and engagement, move the headline KPI cards to those aggregates, and add date-window plus pagination or cursor inputs for delivery and campaign list views. Add the missing leading foreign-key indexes on the `admin_email_*` reporting tables, and relabel opens as a partial or proxy signal while prioritizing delivered, failed, clicked, replied, or conversion-linked metrics for decision-making.
- Acceptance criteria: Admin email summary cards read aggregate data instead of a capped sample; delivery and campaign tables support bounded filters or pagination; the targeted `admin_email_*` foreign-key indexes exist or are explicitly waived with reason; UI copy no longer presents opens as a decision-grade engagement metric.

## Business Access And Reporting Rules

- Client Finance payment and invoice reporting: Client Finance needs company-scoped finance-safe payment, invoice, and exception reporting, but should not receive operational relationship data such as target-contact identity, Bum IDs, or unrelated reporter identity. Sensitivity: financial plus operational workflow context. Add or clarify in `docs/business-access-rules.md`: a finance-safe read model for Client Finance, with richer operational joins reserved for Client Admin or Admin. Verification: response-shape tests for Client Finance plus positive-role coverage for Admin and Client Admin.
- Admin email operations and reporting: Admins need raw template, campaign, delivery, suppression, and engagement data to operate marketplace communications and troubleshoot sends. Client Admins, Client Finance, Client Members, Bums, and Public Visitors should not see recipient-level rows, message body snapshots, suppression state, or recipient engagement history. Sensitivity: high, because the data includes recipient identity, content snapshots, and behavior signals. Add or clarify in `docs/business-access-rules.md`: an explicit admin-only rule for raw email operations and reporting. Verification: route/API negative-role tests plus admin-only positive coverage.
- Shared email KPIs outside admin: If leadership or client-facing reporting ever needs communication metrics, those users should receive aggregate delivery, failure, click, reply, or conversion metrics only, not raw recipient rows or message bodies. Sensitivity: medium to high depending on segmentation. Add or clarify in `docs/business-access-rules.md`: aggregate-only exception rules and the fields that must stay excluded. Verification: response-shape assertions and column-level export/report checks.

## Watchlist

- Terms-acceptance reporting still selects full `terms_versions.body` and `faq_body` through `listTermsAcceptances()`, and live data still shows up to `5,970` body characters and `2,464` FAQ characters per version. That overfetch is real, but with only `19` acceptance rows across `5` versions it is lower leverage than the active finance and admin-email gaps unless admin report latency worsens or a non-admin reporting surface is introduced.
- Raw telemetry volume has grown to `103,983` `performance_metric_events` rows, but the current analytics surface is improved: source uses `admin_performance_metric_summary` and `admin_performance_route_summary` instead of browser-side p75 math, and direct SQL still shows the heaviest recent LCP routes in the low-seconds range. Keep further telemetry aggregation or retention work with Performance unless `/admin/performance` latency, report needs, or data-retention policy changes reopen it for Data/Analytics.
- `admin_scrum_items` is now present in live Supabase with admin-only RLS and zero current rows. Do not add a new analytics recommendation yet; revisit only if the tracker becomes a shared reporting/export surface or accumulates enough operational history to require aggregate reporting rules.
