- Evidence: Current source still starts major client and Bum shells from broad list reads even though the live datasets remain tiny. `src/pages/client/ClientDashboard.tsx` still boots from `listOpportunityRegistrations()`, `listCustomerPaymentReports()`, `listClaimInvoices()`, `listClientReverseOpportunities()`, and `listCustomerTargetResponses()`. `src/pages/client/ClientReports.tsx`, `src/pages/client/ClientExports.tsx`, and `src/pages/client/ClientOpportunityNew.tsx` still hydrate the same broad helper set, while `src/pages/bum/BumDashboard.tsx` still loads marketplace opportunities, prospect recommendations, reverse opportunities, own profile, and `useIntroClaims()` just to render summary cards, `src/pages/bum/BumOpportunities.tsx` still pulls `listMarketplaceOpportunities()`, `listCustomerTargets(null)`, `listBumSavedItems()`, `listBumRepresentedContacts()`, and claim summaries together, and `src/pages/bum/BumLiveConversations.tsx` still loads `listConversationThreads()`, `listCustomerTargets(null)`, and `listTeamsMeetings()` on entry. The shared helpers in `src/lib/portalApi.ts` still select broad company or whole-table arrays with no pagination or route-scoped bounds for `listOpportunityRegistrations()`, `listMarketplaceOpportunities()`, `listOwnOpportunityRegistrations()`, `listOpportunityClaims()`, `listCustomerTargets()`, `listCustomerPaymentReports()`, `listClaimInvoices()`, `listClientReverseOpportunities()`, `listTeamsMeetings()`, and `listCustomerTargetResponses()`. Live 30-day telemetry now keeps `/client/dashboard` at `2428 ms` p75 LCP with `10,622` samples, `/bum/dashboard` at `2408 ms` with `4,907`, `/bum/profile` at `2312 ms` with `205`, `/client/opportunities/new` at `2220 ms` with `519`, `/bum/live-conversations` at `2220 ms` with `418`, `/client/opportunities` at `2212 ms` with `276`, and `/bum/opportunities` at `2196 ms` with `828` while the backing tables remain tiny (`83` opportunity registrations, `81` customer targets, `1` opportunity claim, `2` bum contacts, and `0` current rows each in `customer_target_responses`, `reverse_opportunities`, `teams_meetings`, `customer_payment_reports`, and `claim_invoices`).
- Why it matters: These route shells are still paying first-render cost out of proportion to the live dataset size. If they keep hydrating broad lists up front, latency will scale with marketplace growth instead of with the narrow slice each screen actually needs.
- Recommendation: Replace whole-list route-start reads with server-scoped summaries, capped or paginated lists, and on-demand detail fetches for the shared client and Bum shells first. Keep report, export, finance, and marketplace detail payloads off first render, and narrow the shared helpers so dashboard, opportunity, conversation, and profile routes stop hydrating unrelated records.
- Acceptance criteria: `/client/dashboard`, `/client/opportunities`, `/client/opportunities/new`, `/client/reports`, `/client/payments`, `/client/exports`, `/bum/dashboard`, `/bum/opportunities`, and `/bum/live-conversations` no longer depend on broad list hydration for first render; the shared helpers request only route-scoped or paginated data; and follow-up field telemetry shows lower transfer and improved LCP on the current client and Bum hotspots.
