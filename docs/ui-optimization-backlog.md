### P1 - [TB-0110] Explain the sponsor-call gate before disabling Bum claim submission
- Evidence: Current exact-head source in [BumOpportunities.tsx](/Users/macdaddy/CodexWork/TrustedBums/trustedbums/src/pages/bum/BumOpportunities.tsx) and [BumOpportunityDetail.tsx](/Users/macdaddy/CodexWork/TrustedBums/trustedbums/src/pages/bum/BumOpportunityDetail.tsx) now requires a binary `I can sponsor a call with this customer` choice before the claim CTA enables, and [portalApi.ts](/Users/macdaddy/CodexWork/TrustedBums/trustedbums/src/lib/portalApi.ts) now rejects claim creation when `canSponsorCall` is false. Exact-head hosted visual run `27671724557` completed successfully, but its captured Bum claim interaction view still stops above the new sponsorship field, so this item remains primarily source-backed rather than screenshot-backed. Current W3C guidance still says form controls need labels and instructions that make the expected choice clear: [Labels or Instructions](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG21/Understanding/labels-or-instructions.html) and [WAI form instructions](https://www.w3.org/WAI/tutorials/forms/instructions/).
- Why it matters: This is a workflow-eligibility rule, not a low-stakes preference. Right now the UI turns it into an unexplained binary gate, which risks confusion, distrust, and silent abandonment when the CTA stays disabled.
- Recommendation: Replace the generic yes/no phrasing with a clearer acknowledgment pattern and persistent helper text that explains what qualifies as sponsorship, why the answer matters, and what the Bum should do if the answer is no. Mirror the same guidance in both claim entry points.
- Acceptance criteria: Both `/bum/opportunities` claim dialog and `/bum/opportunities/:id` claim form show visible helper text tied to the field. The control language makes the requirement self-explanatory, and the disabled or blocked CTA explains why submission cannot continue without call sponsorship.
