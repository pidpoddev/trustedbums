### P1 - Turn the mobile reports workspace into progressive disclosure instead of a full control stack
- Evidence: The latest available mobile report screenshots show Admin, Client Finance, and Bum report pages spending multiple phone viewports on report cards, date range, and full field checklists before users reach the first live chart or table preview in [mobile-chrome-admin-admin-reports.png](/Users/macdaddy/tmp/trustedbums-visual-ui-27083467531/test-results/visual-ui-audit-authentica-1b0bc-portal-pages-render-cleanly-mobile-chrome/mobile-chrome-admin-admin-reports.png), [mobile-chrome-client_finance-client-finance-reports.png](/Users/macdaddy/tmp/trustedbums-visual-ui-27083467531/test-results/visual-ui-audit-authentica-c1452-portal-pages-render-cleanly-mobile-chrome/mobile-chrome-client_finance-client-finance-reports.png), and [mobile-chrome-bum-bum-reports.png](/Users/macdaddy/tmp/trustedbums-visual-ui-27083467531/test-results/visual-ui-audit-authentica-0c868-portal-pages-render-cleanly-mobile-chrome/mobile-chrome-bum-bum-reports.png). The shared layout in [ReportsWorkspace.tsx](/Users/macdaddy/CodexWork/TrustedBums/trustedbums/src/components/reports/ReportsWorkspace.tsx) still renders the full report selector, date control, and visible-field checklist ahead of the preview on narrow screens. The June 8 report refactor changed report data/model files, not this shared mobile layout.
- Why it matters: Reporting pages should answer quick operational questions. On mobile, the current control stack makes reporting feel like setup work before users can even scan a result.
- Recommendation: On narrow screens, collapse report selection and field toggles into progressive disclosure patterns such as an accordion, drawer, or sticky “Customize report” sheet. Keep the selected report summary, export action, metrics, and first preview block visible within the first viewport or short scroll.
- Acceptance criteria: Mobile screenshots for `/admin/reports`, `/client/reports`, and `/bum/reports` show the active report summary and first chart/table preview before a long settings scroll. Field toggles are collapsed by default, and users can still reach export plus key metrics without passing through the full checkbox list first.

- Evidence: [mobile-chrome-public-privacy-policy.png](/Users/macdaddy/tmp/trustedbums-visual-ui-27083467531/test-results/visual-ui-audit-public-vis-0a461-ivacy-states-render-cleanly-mobile-chrome/mobile-chrome-public-privacy-policy.png) still shows a tiny left-edge `Privacy` reopen chip. - Recommendation: Replace the tiny reopen chip with a normal-size `Privacy choices` pill/button that fits the coordinated utility pattern above, then extend [visual-ui-audit.spec.ts](/Users/macdaddy/CodexWork/TrustedBums/trustedbums/tests/e2e/visual-ui-audit.spec.ts) to capture `/privacy-policy?consent=open` or an equivalent reopened state from the legal route itself.
- Acceptance criteria: The dismissed consent entry point is visibly tappable on mobile without precision aiming, and the next `visual-ui-audit` artifact includes a privacy-page screenshot with consent controls reopened from the legal page context.

### P1 - Reframe public legal links and policy content as mobile trust navigation
- Evidence: [mobile-chrome-public-privacy-policy.png](/Users/macdaddy/tmp/trustedbums-visual-ui-27083467531/test-results/visual-ui-audit-public-vis-0a461-ivacy-states-render-cleanly-mobile-chrome/mobile-chrome-public-privacy-policy.png) still shows `Related Legal Links` as a dense wrapped link cluster below the consent card, followed by a long undifferentiated section stack. Source still uses a simple wrap layout for legal links and long-form sections in [PrivacyPolicy.tsx](/Users/macdaddy/CodexWork/TrustedBums/trustedbums/src/pages/PrivacyPolicy.tsx).
- Why it matters: Privacy and legal pages are part of the public trust journey. On phones, the current presentation reads like compressed fine print instead of structured, intentional guidance.
- Recommendation: Turn related legal destinations into stacked rows, pills, or short accordions with clearer hierarchy, and add stronger section dividers or compact in-page navigation so the privacy policy scans like a trust surface rather than one long wall of text.
- Acceptance criteria: Mobile legal links no longer wrap into a dense inline cluster, each destination has a comfortable tap area, and the privacy page exposes visibly distinct section groups or navigation anchors that improve scanability.

### P1 - Stop showing placeholder finance totals on the Bum dashboard
- Evidence: [mobile-chrome-bum-bum-dashboard.png](/Users/macdaddy/tmp/trustedbums-visual-ui-27083467531/test-results/visual-ui-audit-authentica-0c868-portal-pages-render-cleanly-mobile-chrome/mobile-chrome-bum-bum-dashboard.png) still shows `Pending Earnings` and `Lifetime Payouts` as `$0`, while [BumDashboard.tsx](/Users/macdaddy/CodexWork/TrustedBums/trustedbums/src/pages/bum/BumDashboard.tsx) still hardcodes those values and [BumEarnings.tsx](/Users/macdaddy/CodexWork/TrustedBums/trustedbums/src/pages/bum/BumEarnings.tsx) already computes live payout totals.
- Why it matters: A Bum-facing payout summary that looks placeholder-driven undermines trust in the earning model even when the detailed earnings page already has live logic.
- Recommendation: Reuse the same payout aggregation on the dashboard that powers `/bum/earnings`, or explicitly label the cards as unavailable until live totals are loaded there.
- Acceptance criteria: Dashboard payout cards either match the `/bum/earnings` totals logic or clearly communicate an intentional unavailable state. Loading, empty, and true-zero states are visually distinct.
