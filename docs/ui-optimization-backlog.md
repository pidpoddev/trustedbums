## Active Recommendations

### P1 - [TB-0061] Turn mobile reports into progressive disclosure instead of a full control stack
- Evidence: Exact-head [mobile-chrome-client_finance-client-finance-reports.png](/private/tmp/trustedbums-visual-27488973899-20260615/test-results/visual-ui-audit-authentica-c1452-portal-pages-render-cleanly-mobile-chrome/mobile-chrome-client_finance-client-finance-reports.png) still spends most of the opening mobile viewport on report selection, date range, and field toggles before the first useful preview appears. The retained artifact metadata for `/client/reports` still shows a `1972px` full-page scroll height on a `412x839` viewport, and current shared source in [ReportsWorkspace.tsx](/Users/macdaddy/CodexWork/TrustedBums/trustedbums/src/components/reports/ReportsWorkspace.tsx) still renders the full chooser, date selector, and visible-field checklist ahead of the preview on narrow screens. W3C’s current accordion guidance still positions accordions as the pattern for reducing stacked-scroll setup: [WAI APG accordion pattern](https://www.w3.org/WAI/ARIA/apg/patterns/accordion/).
- Why it matters: Reports should answer a question quickly. On mobile, the current sequence still feels like setup work before results.
- Recommendation: Collapse report selection and field toggles into progressive disclosure on narrow screens, such as an accordion, sheet, or drawer, while keeping export, selected-report context, top metrics, and the first preview block within the first viewport or short scroll.
- Acceptance criteria: Mobile screenshots for `/admin/reports`, `/client/reports`, and `/bum/reports` show the active report summary and first chart or table preview before a long settings stack. Field toggles are collapsed by default, and export remains reachable without scrolling through the whole checklist.

### P1 - [TB-0062] Rebuild privacy and legal mobile controls as trust navigation, not micro-utilities
- Evidence: Exact-head [mobile-chrome-public-privacy-policy.png](/private/tmp/trustedbums-visual-27488973899-20260615/test-results/visual-ui-audit-public-vis-0a461-ivacy-states-render-cleanly-mobile-chrome/mobile-chrome-public-privacy-policy.png) still shows a tiny bottom-left `Privacy` reopen chip plus a dense wrapped `Related Legal Links` cluster on the public trust route. Current source still renders the closed-state entry point as `h-5` with `text-[10px]` in [ConsentManager.tsx](/Users/macdaddy/CodexWork/TrustedBums/trustedbums/src/components/ConsentManager.tsx), and [PrivacyPolicy.tsx](/Users/macdaddy/CodexWork/TrustedBums/trustedbums/src/pages/PrivacyPolicy.tsx) still relies on a wrapped inline legal-link row. WCAG 2.2 still sets a `24 by 24 CSS pixels` minimum target size for pointer inputs: [W3C target size minimum](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/target-size-minimum.html).
- Why it matters: Privacy and legal routes are public trust surfaces. Tiny reopen controls and cramped legal navigation still make these pages feel improvised instead of deliberate and credible.
- Recommendation: Replace the tiny reopen chip with a normal-size `Privacy choices` button or pill that matches the shared support pattern, and restyle related legal destinations into stacked rows, grouped pills, or compact accordions with clearer section rhythm on mobile.
- Acceptance criteria: The dismissed consent entry point is visibly tappable without precision aiming, legal destinations no longer wrap into a cramped inline cluster, and the privacy page exposes separated section groups or navigation that scans like an intentional trust surface.
