### P1 - Replace the tiny consent reopen tab and keep privacy controls unobscured on mobile
- Evidence: Fresh Pixel 7 screenshots from this run still show the dismissed consent control as a tiny fixed `Privacy` tab on the left edge of the homepage and privacy page ([mobile-chrome-public-home.png](</Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted Bums LOCAL/trustedbums/test-results/visual-ui-audit-public-vis-0a461-ivacy-states-render-cleanly-mobile-chrome/mobile-chrome-public-home.png>), [mobile-chrome-public-privacy-policy.png](</Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted Bums LOCAL/trustedbums/test-results/visual-ui-audit-public-vis-0a461-ivacy-states-render-cleanly-mobile-chrome/mobile-chrome-public-privacy-policy.png>)). A fresh direct capture of `/privacy-policy?consent=reset` shows the open consent sheet obscuring the page’s own `Manage Consent` card and pushing `Related Legal Links` underneath the overlay ([privacy-policy-consent-reset-mobile-2026-05-31.png](</Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted Bums LOCAL/trustedbums/tmp-ui-audit/privacy-policy-consent-reset-mobile-2026-05-31.png>)). The implementation still renders the closed-state trigger at `h-5` with `text-[10px]` in [src/components/ConsentManager.tsx](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/src/components/ConsentManager.tsx:141). Current guidance still sets a 24x24 CSS pixel minimum target in WCAG 2.2 and recommends larger mobile controls plus spacing in Apple’s HIG accessibility guidance.
- Why it matters: Consent is a front-door trust signal. When the reopen affordance looks accidental and the open sheet covers the product’s own legal controls, the interface feels defensive instead of deliberate.
- Recommendation: Replace the closed-state tab with a larger `Privacy choices` chip or button that clears page content and browser chrome on mobile. Present the open state as a contained bottom sheet that preserves visibility of the privacy page’s own consent and legal modules instead of stacking directly over them.
- Acceptance criteria: On mobile widths, the dismissed consent entry point meets at least the WCAG 2.2 minimum target expectation and is visually intentional; opening consent does not cover the privacy page’s `Manage Consent` card or `Related Legal Links`; the same control pattern works cleanly on public and authenticated pages.

### P1 - Turn public legal links into grouped mobile navigation
- Evidence: Fresh mobile screenshots still show homepage footer links and privacy-page legal destinations collapsing into dense multi-line text clusters ([mobile-chrome-public-home.png](</Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted Bums LOCAL/trustedbums/test-results/visual-ui-audit-public-vis-0a461-ivacy-states-render-cleanly-mobile-chrome/mobile-chrome-public-home.png>), [mobile-chrome-public-privacy-policy.png](</Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted Bums LOCAL/trustedbums/test-results/visual-ui-audit-public-vis-0a461-ivacy-states-render-cleanly-mobile-chrome/mobile-chrome-public-privacy-policy.png>)). Source confirms that the homepage footer still renders every legal destination in a single wrapping row in [src/pages/Index.tsx](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/src/pages/Index.tsx:662) and the privacy page mirrors that approach in [src/pages/PrivacyPolicy.tsx](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/src/pages/PrivacyPolicy.tsx:121). Current touch-target and spacing guidance from WCAG 2.2 and Apple HIG still make this density risky for scanning and tap accuracy.
- Why it matters: These links are part of the site’s legitimacy proof. On phones they still read like compressed fine print instead of intentional trust navigation.
- Recommendation: Group legal destinations into clearer mobile clusters such as `Privacy`, `Terms`, and `Operations`, then render them as stacked rows, pill links, or compact accordions with stronger section labels and breathing room.
- Acceptance criteria: On mobile, legal destinations are visually grouped instead of appearing as a wrapped word cloud; each item has a comfortable tap area; the homepage footer and privacy page use the same grouping pattern.

### P1 - Replace hardcoded Bum earnings KPIs with live totals or an explicit pending state
- Evidence: Fresh live Bum dashboard screenshots from this run still show `Pending Earnings` and `Lifetime Payouts` locked at `$0` even while the same dashboard shows live activity such as `97` open opportunities and `1` intro request ([chromium-bum-bum-dashboard.png](</Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted Bums LOCAL/trustedbums/test-results/visual-ui-audit-authentica-0c868-portal-pages-render-cleanly-chromium/chromium-bum-bum-dashboard.png>)). The dashboard implementation still hardcodes those two cards in [src/pages/bum/BumDashboard.tsx](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/src/pages/bum/BumDashboard.tsx:232), while [src/pages/bum/BumEarnings.tsx](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/src/pages/bum/BumEarnings.tsx:15) already computes pending and paid payout totals from live data.
- Why it matters: Static finance metrics degrade trust fast because they look like placeholders rather than product state. That is especially costly on the Bum dashboard, where payout confidence is part of product credibility.
- Recommendation: Feed the dashboard cards from the same totals used on `/bum/earnings`, or replace them with an explicit earnings-status module until live payout data is intentionally unavailable.
- Acceptance criteria: Dashboard earnings metrics match `/bum/earnings`, or the dashboard clearly communicates that payout totals are not yet available; loading and empty states are visibly different from true zero balances.

### P1 - Restore current-route context in mobile authenticated headers
- Evidence: The active-route label is still hidden below `sm` in [src/layouts/AdminLayout.tsx](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/src/layouts/AdminLayout.tsx:131), [src/layouts/ClientLayout.tsx](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/src/layouts/ClientLayout.tsx:126), and [src/layouts/BumLayout.tsx](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/src/layouts/BumLayout.tsx:96). Fresh desktop role screenshots from this run confirm the header pattern is otherwise stable across Admin, Client Admin, Client Finance, and Bum pages, but the current live mobile audit still covers only public routes, not collapsed authenticated shells.
- Why it matters: The current-route cue disappears at the same breakpoint where the sidebar collapses, which makes quick mobile checks feel less anchored for every authenticated role.
- Recommendation: Add a compact below-`sm` route label beside the sidebar trigger or in a second thin header row that coexists with search and account controls across all three portal shells.
- Acceptance criteria: On mobile authenticated routes, users can identify the current page without opening the sidebar; the label remains readable beside existing controls; no header clipping or wrap regressions appear on narrow widths.

### P2 - Rebalance the six-card KPI grids on client and Bum dashboards
- Evidence: Fresh live desktop screenshots from this run still show both six-card KPI sections breaking into an uneven `4 + 2` composition on Client Admin and Bum dashboards ([chromium-client_admin-client-admin-dashboard.png](</Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted Bums LOCAL/trustedbums/test-results/visual-ui-audit-authentica-71083-portal-pages-render-cleanly-chromium/chromium-client_admin-client-admin-dashboard.png>), [chromium-bum-bum-dashboard.png](</Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted Bums LOCAL/trustedbums/test-results/visual-ui-audit-authentica-0c868-portal-pages-render-cleanly-chromium/chromium-bum-bum-dashboard.png>)). The implementations still render six equal-weight cards inside `lg:grid-cols-4` in [src/pages/client/ClientDashboard.tsx](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/src/pages/client/ClientDashboard.tsx:252) and [src/pages/bum/BumDashboard.tsx](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/src/pages/bum/BumDashboard.tsx:227).
- Why it matters: These dashboards are first-scan surfaces. The stranded second row makes the composition feel unfinished and weakens metric hierarchy.
- Recommendation: Move these KPI groups to a balanced desktop composition such as `xl:grid-cols-3`, or split the cards into primary and secondary groups if certain metrics deserve stronger emphasis.
- Acceptance criteria: On large screens, both KPI sections render in balanced rows with no stranded two-card row; card heights stay aligned within each row; focus order still follows visual reading order.
