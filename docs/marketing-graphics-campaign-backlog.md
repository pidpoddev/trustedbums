## Reusable Visual System

- Approved motifs: guarded route, buyer briefing board, protected review card, selective checkpoint, abstract approval marker, serious dossier framing.
- Layout rules: one focal route only; preserve obvious copy-safe space; keep every panel abstract enough that users never expect readable UI text inside the art.
- Color and typography direction: deep ink `#08111f`, warm cream `#fbf5eb` to `#fff8ef`, action orange `#ff7a1a` to `#f15a16`, and restrained teal accents. Editable overlay typography should stay in repo-approved `Space Grotesk` plus `Inter`.
- Overlay guidance that is working: lead with the buyer objection or homepage thesis, keep headlines under roughly LinkedIn's current 70-character guidance, and keep support copy short enough that the image still reads first.
- Negative prompts: `no baked-in text`, `no pseudo-text`, `no fake notifications`, `no non-functional buttons`, `no customer logos`, `no handshake stock`, `no smiling sales-team stock`, `no AI glow`, `no watermark signatures`.
- Text handling rule: Trusted Bums brand text, headlines, CTAs, proof points, and any legal-sensitive wording stay in editable vector, HTML/CSS, or approved text layers rather than inside raster artwork.
- Current platform and accessibility guardrails: LinkedIn single-image ads still support horizontal `1.91:1`, square `1:1`, and vertical `4:5` with vertical delivery optimized for mobile; Meta photo-ad guidance still favors a single focal point, minimal image text, consistent campaign visuals, and previewing the ad before launch; Google still flags image ads that mimic dialogs, notifications, or standalone buttons without context; WCAG 2.2 still requires at least `4.5:1` contrast for normal text overlays. Sources: [LinkedIn single image ads](https://business.linkedin.com/marketing-solutions/success/ads-guide/single-image-ads?src=bl-po), [LinkedIn single image ads overview](https://www.linkedin.com/help/linkedin/answer/a420760), [Meta photo ads](https://www.facebook.com/business/ads/photo-ad-format), [Google misleading ad design](https://support.google.com/adspolicy/answer/15937463?hl=en), [W3C contrast minimum](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/contrast-minimum).
