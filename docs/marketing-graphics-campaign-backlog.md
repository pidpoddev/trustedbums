## Reusable Visual System

- Approved motifs: structured client brief, protected warm-intro route, dark buyer-review lane, separate muted secondary lane, checkpoint card, pale follow-up card, restrained orange-plus-teal contrast.
- Layout rules: keep one dominant orange Client path; keep any secondary lane visually quieter; preserve large copy-safe space; keep every plate abstract enough that the audience never expects readable UI labels inside the art.
- Color and typography direction: deep ink `#08111f` to `#12253d`, warm cream `#f9f2e8` to `#fbf6ee`, action orange `#ff7317` to `#ff9637`, and muted teal `#7fb7ad` to `#178b83`. Editable overlays should stay in repo-approved `Space Grotesk` plus `Inter`.
- Overlay guidance that is working: lead with the buyer problem or path separation, keep the headline short enough for mobile crops, and avoid internal jargon like `Client Prospect` in public-facing campaign copy.
- Negative prompts: `no baked-in text`, `no pseudo-text`, `no fake notifications`, `no fake buttons`, `no customer logos`, `no handshake stock`, `no smiling SDR team stock`, `no AI glow`, `no watermark signatures`.
- Text handling rule: Trusted Bums brand text, headlines, CTAs, proof points, statistics, and legal-sensitive wording stay in editable vector, HTML/CSS, or approved text layers rather than raster artwork.
- Current platform and accessibility guardrails: LinkedIn still supports single-image ads in horizontal `1.91:1`, square `1:1`, and vertical `4:5`; Meta still recommends a single focal point and previewing photo-ad crops before launch; Google still flags image ads that mimic dialogs, notifications, or standalone buttons; WCAG 2.2 still requires at least `4.5:1` contrast for normal text overlays. Sources: [LinkedIn single image ads](https://business.linkedin.com/marketing-solutions/success/ads-guide/single-image-ads?src=bl-po), [LinkedIn single image ads overview](https://www.linkedin.com/help/linkedin/answer/a420760), [Meta photo ads](https://www.facebook.com/business/ads/photo-ad-format), [Google misleading ad design](https://support.google.com/adspolicy/answer/15937463?hl=en), [W3C contrast minimum](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/contrast-minimum).
