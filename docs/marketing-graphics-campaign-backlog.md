## Reusable Visual System

- Approved motifs: buyer briefing board, protected review card, trust checkpoint, single warm route, abstract approval marker, high-value dossier framing.
- Layout rules: one route only; preserve large left-side or upper-left copy-safe zones; keep every surface abstract enough that viewers do not expect readable UI text.
- Color and typography direction: deep ink `#08111f`, warm cream `#fbf5eb` to `#fff8ef`, action orange `#ff7a1a` to `#f15a16`, and restrained trust teal `#178b83`; overlay typography should stay editable and use `Space Grotesk` with `Inter` per [`src/index.css`](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/src/index.css).
- Prompt fragments that are working: `text-free B2B ad plate`, `executive proof room`, `buyer briefing board`, `protected review surface`, `credible route`, `single focal path`, `no fake UI labels`.
- Negative prompts: `no baked-in text`, `no pseudo-text`, `no customer logos`, `no fake badges with words`, `no buttons`, `no dialog boxes`, `no handshake stock`, `no smiling sales team`, `no AI glow`, `no watermark signatures`.
- Text handling rule: Trusted Bums logo text, headlines, CTAs, claims, statistics, and legal-sensitive wording must stay in editable vector, HTML/CSS, SVG text, or approved brand assets rather than baked into bitmaps.
- Policy and accessibility guardrails: LinkedIn still recommends `1200 x 628` landscape and `1200 x 1200` square across desktop and mobile, while vertical single-image creative is mobile-only; Meta still advises minimal image text, one focal point, consistent campaign visuals, and previewing the ad before launch; Google still disallows misleading ad designs that mimic notifications, dialogs, or non-functional buttons; WCAG 2.2 still requires at least `4.5:1` contrast for normal text overlays. Sources: [LinkedIn single image ads](https://business.linkedin.com/marketing-solutions/success/ads-guide/single-image-ads?src=bl-po), [Meta photo ads](https://www.facebook.com/business/ads/photo-ad-format), [Google misleading ad design](https://support.google.com/adspolicy/answer/15937463?hl=en), [W3C contrast minimum](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/contrast-minimum).
