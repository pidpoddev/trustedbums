_Last updated: 2026-06-04 by Codex daily marketing graphics artist automation._

## Executive Creative Direction

Today's strongest angle is trust-building acquisition for skeptical B2B buyers and client admins who may assume Trusted Bums is another noisy lead-gen funnel. The creative system should show selective access instead of volume: one credible path through clutter, one connector, one guarded account, and enough negative space that editable overlays can stay short, calm, and serious across LinkedIn, paid social, and email.

## Reusable Visual System

- Approved motifs: guarded account door, one trusted route, outreach clutter reduced to abstract cards and lines, centered connector, calm buyer conversation card.
- Layout rules: maintain one focal path only; preserve large negative-space copy zones; keep any card-like shapes abstract enough that viewers do not expect readable UI text.
- Color and typography direction: deep ink background or frame, warm cream panels, action orange as the route emphasis, trust teal as a minor confirmation signal, and editable overlays set in Space Grotesk plus Inter only.
- Prompt fragments that are working: `text-free marketing plate`, `selective access`, `one trusted route`, `guarded buyer`, `calm conversation card`, `no fake UI labels`.
- Negative prompts: `no baked-in text`, `no pseudo-text`, `no customer logos`, `no fake badges`, `no handshake stock`, `no smiling sales team stock`, `no generic SaaS dashboard`, `no AI glow`, `no watermarks`.
- Text handling rule: Trusted Bums logo, headlines, CTA copy, proof points, and legal-sensitive language must stay in editable vector, HTML/CSS, SVG text, or existing approved logo assets rather than baked into generated bitmaps.
- QA reminder: Existing public narrative SVGs such as `public/brand-blocked-inbox.svg`, `public/brand-trust-connector.svg`, `public/brand-revenue-loop.svg`, and `public/og-image.svg` contain visible words. They are editable and brand-aligned, but they still require spelling-aware visual review before campaign reuse.
