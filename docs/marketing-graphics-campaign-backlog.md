## Reusable Visual System

- Approved motifs: qualification brief, reviewed proof gate, dark account-review panel, pale evidence card, one dominant orange route, one quiet teal support lane, restrained cream-paper background, and abstract trust signals that never read as clickable UI.
- Layout rules: keep one dominant Client route, make the review gate the visual hinge, preserve large copy-safe space, and keep every plate abstract enough that users do not expect readable in-art interface text.
- Color and typography direction: deep ink `#08111f` to `#13263f`, warm cream `#f8f1e7` to `#fbf6ee`, action orange `#ff7317` to `#ff9637`, and muted teal `#7fb7ad` to `#178b83`. Editable overlays should stay in `Space Grotesk` plus `Inter`, matching the shipped public brand system in `src/index.css`.
- Overlay guidance that is working: lead with the live Client-page language around blocked buyers, trust, warm routes, hard accounts, access alignment, review, draft, or publish; keep the headline short enough for mobile crops; avoid internal nouns like `Client Prospect`; and keep the CTA explicitly about strategy review or intro strategy rather than broad conversion.
- Proof rule: use `docs/claim-safe-proof-spine.md` as the live public-proof boundary. Graphics may imply controlled review, qualification, and route testing, but may not imply guaranteed meetings, guaranteed access, guaranteed revenue, or unapproved customer proof.
- Negative prompts: `no baked-in text`, `no pseudo-text`, `no fake notifications`, `no fake buttons`, `no customer logos`, `no fake dashboards`, `no handshake stock`, `no AI glow`, `no watermark signatures`.
- Text handling rule: Trusted Bums brand text, headlines, CTAs, proof points, statistics, and legal-sensitive wording stay in editable vector, HTML/CSS, or approved text layers rather than raster artwork.
- Current platform and accessibility guardrails: LinkedIn’s current single-image specs still recommend `1200 x 628` for `1.91:1`, `1200 x 1200` for `1:1`, and support `4:5` vertical delivery for mobile; LinkedIn’s current Sponsored Content guidance still favors concise headlines and focused creative; Meta’s current placement guidance still supports and recommends `4:5` single-image creative for feed-heavy use; Google’s current misleading-ad-design policy still restricts standalone buttons and deceptive interface-like controls in image ads; and WCAG 2.2 still requires at least `4.5:1` contrast for normal text overlays. Sources: [LinkedIn single image ad specs](https://business.linkedin.com/advertise/ads/sponsored-content/single-image-ads-specs), [LinkedIn Sponsored Content tips](https://business.linkedin.com/advertise/ads/best-practices/sponsored-content-tips), [Meta aspect-ratio guidance](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/103816146375741), [Google misleading ad design](https://support.google.com/adspolicy/answer/15937463?hl=en), [WCAG 2.2](https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG22/).
