## Reusable Visual System

- Approved motifs: qualification brief, reviewed proof gate, dark account-review panel, pale evidence card, one dominant orange route, one quiet teal support lane, restrained cream-paper background, and abstract trust cues that never read as clickable UI.
- Layout rules: keep one dominant Client route, make the review gate the visual hinge, preserve generous copy-safe space, and keep every plate abstract enough that users do not expect readable in-art interface text.
- Color and typography direction: deep ink `#08111f` to `#13263f`, warm cream `#f8f1e7` to `#fbf6ee`, action orange `#ff7317` to `#ff9637`, and muted teal `#7fb7ad` to `#178b83`. Editable overlays should stay in `Space Grotesk` plus `Inter`, matching the shipped public brand system in `src/index.css`.
- Overlay guidance that is working: use public-story language around ignored strangers, warm routes, hard accounts, access alignment, review, and credible friends; keep headlines short enough for mobile crops; avoid internal product nouns like `Client Prospect`; and keep the CTA about strategy review rather than broad conversion.
- Proof rule: use `docs/claim-safe-proof-spine.md` as the live public-proof boundary. Graphics may imply controlled review, qualification, and route testing, but may not imply guaranteed meetings, guaranteed access, guaranteed revenue, or unapproved customer proof.
- Negative prompts: `no baked-in text`, `no pseudo-text`, `no fake notifications`, `no fake buttons`, `no customer logos`, `no fake dashboards`, `no handshake stock`, `no AI glow`, `no watermark signatures`.
- Text handling rule: Trusted Bums brand text, headlines, CTAs, proof points, statistics, and legal-sensitive wording stay in editable vector, HTML/CSS, or approved text layers rather than raster artwork.
- Current platform and accessibility guardrails: LinkedIn still recommends `1200 x 628` for `1.91:1` single-image delivery and `1200 x 1200` for `1:1`; Meta still supports both `1:1` and `4:5` in feed placements and recommends vertical `4:5` for single-image feed use; Google still restricts misleading ad design such as non-functional buttons or UI-like controls; and WCAG 2.2 still requires at least `4.5:1` contrast for normal text overlays. Sources: [LinkedIn single image ad specs](https://business.linkedin.com/advertise/ads/sponsored-content/single-image-ads-specs), [Meta feed aspect-ratio guidance](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/103816146375741), [Google misleading ad design](https://support.google.com/adspolicy/answer/15937463?hl=en), [WCAG 2.2](https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG22/).
