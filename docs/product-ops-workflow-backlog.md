## Active Recommendations

### P0 - Restrict Bum represented contacts to entitled destinations only
- Evidence: [`portal-contacts`](/Users/macdaddy/CodexWork/TrustedBums/trustedbums/supabase/functions/portal-contacts/index.ts:474) lists every accepted opportunity as a contact-link option. [`contactPayloadFromPatch`](/Users/macdaddy/CodexWork/TrustedBums/trustedbums/supabase/functions/portal-contacts/index.ts:527) validates only that destination IDs exist before writing them onto `bum_contacts`. The business rule in [`docs/business-access-rules.md`](/Users/macdaddy/CodexWork/TrustedBums/trustedbums/docs/business-access-rules.md:291) says Bums may create captures and represented contacts only for destinations they are allowed to access. Supabase performance advisors also still flag multiple permissive policies on `public.opportunity_registrations`, reinforcing that accepted-opportunity visibility is broad enough to deserve workflow-level scoping, not just UI filtering.
- Why it matters: Bums can currently discover or attach represented-contact evidence to unrelated accepted opportunities or client targets. That weakens marketplace boundaries, creates data-trust problems for later client-visible workflows, and makes support rescue harder because operators cannot assume every represented-contact link was legitimate.
- Recommendation: Limit destination options and server-side validation to opportunities and targets the Bum is explicitly entitled to work. Treat represented-contact linking as a privilege derived from an accepted claim, assigned target workflow, accepted intro request, accepted target-response path, or another explicitly documented Bum relationship.
- Acceptance criteria: Bum contact create, update, and detail flows expose only entitled destinations; unrelated opportunity or target IDs are rejected server-side; QA proves one allowed and one denied cross-company or unrelated-destination case; client-visible workflow objects never originate from a represented contact linked to an unauthorized destination.

- Why it matters: The current export surface blends finance-safe payment reporting with operational contact and meeting context. That creates a shipped business-access mismatch and makes support, finance, and future RLS hardening argue over one route instead of over clear export bundles.
- Recommendation: Split `/client/exports` into finance-safe and operational exports. Keep customer payment, commissionable amount, invoice, and payout-adjacent exports available to `CLIENT_FINANCE`. Move target contacts, meeting attendees, join URLs, transcript sync, and any future transcript fields behind `CLIENT_ADMIN` or a future documented finance exception rule.
- Acceptance criteria: `CLIENT_FINANCE` can export finance-safe payment records only; finance users cannot export target contacts, attendee lists, join URLs, transcript fields, or similar operational workflow context by default; QA covers one allowed finance export and one denied operational export.

## Workflow Map

- Profile bootstrap and access: signed-in user declares Client or Bum intent -> verified domain and request type determine auto-domain-claim, same-domain approval, public-email review, related-domain review, or Bum signup review -> Client Admin or Admin approves the allowed request type -> approval updates `profiles`, domain records, and audit trail.
- Public intake and handoffs: public visitor submits contact intent -> anti-abuse and notification path runs -> `contact_submissions` enters admin handoff review -> Admin claims, updates, escalates, converts, or closes the intake.
- Opportunity and relationship work: client, Bum, or admin activity creates opportunity-linked work -> target responses, claims, meetings, transcripts, and conversations attach -> accepted work later feeds payment, invoice, and payout operations.
- Finance flow: customer payment is recorded -> invoice is generated, sent, and marked paid -> payout allocation should follow or enter a finance exception queue.
- Capture and represented-contact flow: Bum capture creates raw relationship evidence -> represented contact must link only to an entitled destination -> any future client-visible output should come from a converted workflow projection, not raw capture rows.
