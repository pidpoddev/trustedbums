### P0 - Split finance-safe exports from operational exports
- Evidence: [`src/App.tsx`](src/App.tsx:151) allows `CLIENT_ADMIN` and `CLIENT_FINANCE` into `/client/exports`. [`src/pages/client/ClientExports.tsx`](src/pages/client/ClientExports.tsx:77) exports target contact names/emails, and [`src/pages/client/ClientExports.tsx`](src/pages/client/ClientExports.tsx:94) exports meeting attendees, `teams_join_url`, and `transcript_sync_status`. [`docs/business-access-rules.md`](docs/business-access-rules.md:322) says Client Finance should not export target contacts, attendee lists, Teams join URLs, transcripts, or operational workflow details by default.
- Why it matters: Finance users currently have a shipped route to operational contact and meeting context that the business rules do not approve. This creates both an access-control defect and a support-policy defect.
- Recommendation: Split `/client/exports` into finance-safe exports and operational exports. Keep customer-payment and invoice-safe exports available to `CLIENT_FINANCE`; move target-contact, meeting-attendee, join-link, transcript, and transcript-status exports behind `CLIENT_ADMIN` or an explicit finance-case participation rule.
- Acceptance criteria: `CLIENT_FINANCE` can export finance-safe payment data only; finance users cannot export target contacts, attendees, Teams join URLs, transcript fields, or meeting operational fields by default; QA covers one allowed finance export and one denied operational export.

## Workflow Map

- Profile bootstrap and access: signed-in user declares Client or Bum intent -> domain and email proof determines auto-claim, same-domain request, public-email company request, related-domain review, or Bum signup review -> Client Admin or Admin approves the allowed request type -> approval changes `profiles.access_status`, `role`, `company_id`, `client_access_role`, or approved company domain -> pending, denied, and disabled users stay out of company data.
- Public intake and handoffs: visitor submits contact form -> abuse checks and notification attempt run -> `contact_submissions` enters admin handoff queue -> Admin owns review, escalation, conversion, reply, or closure.
- Opportunity and relationship work: client, Bum, customer need, or admin action creates opportunity-like work -> claim, intro request, target response, conversation, meeting, and transcript activity attaches -> accepted work eventually drives payment, invoice, and payout events.
- Finance flow: client or admin records customer payment -> invoice is generated, sent, and paid -> payout allocation should follow or enter an exception bucket.
- Capture flow: Bum capture creates raw relationship evidence -> raw capture stays Bum/Admin scoped -> approved conversion creates sanitized contact or workflow context -> client-visible fields depend on the approved converted-field rule.
- Support and transcript rescue: meeting or feedback event occurs -> sync/manual record is expected -> missing, failed, or unresolved item enters admin rescue ownership with age and next action.

## Current Standards And Time-Sensitive Notes

- Zendesk’s SLA guidance centers support operations on visible response/resolution targets, which supports owner, aging, and breach-risk views in admin rescue queues. Source: [Zendesk SLA policies overview](https://support.zendesk.com/hc/en-us/articles/5600997516058-About-SLA-policies-and-how-they-work).
- Stripe Connect documentation ties dispute responsibility to charge type and negative-balance ownership, and Stripe payout reconciliation requires mapping payouts back to included transactions. That supports dedicated finance exception and reconciliation queues rather than flat invoice/payout status lists. Sources: [Stripe Connect disputes](https://docs.stripe.com/disputes/connect), [Stripe payout reconciliation](https://docs.stripe.com/payouts/reconciliation?locale=en-GB).
- Microsoft Graph Teams transcript notifications require subscription lifecycle handling for longer-lived transcript subscriptions and current Teams change-notification docs list transcript notification resource paths. That supports transcript sync monitoring and rescue queues. Sources: [Microsoft Graph transcript notifications](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoftteams/platform/graph-api/meeting-transcripts/fetch-id), [Microsoft Teams change notifications](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/graph/teams-change-notification-in-microsoft-teams-overview), [Microsoft Graph lifecycle notifications](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/graph/change-notifications-lifecycle-events).
