## Active Recommendations

### P0 - Restrict Bum represented contacts to entitled destinations only
- Evidence: [`portal-contacts`](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/supabase/functions/portal-contacts/index.ts:474) lists every `Accepted` opportunity as a link option, and [`contactPayloadFromPatch`](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/supabase/functions/portal-contacts/index.ts:527) accepts any existing `opportunity_registration_id` or [`customer_target_id`](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/supabase/functions/portal-contacts/index.ts:541) after existence checks only. [`docs/business-access-rules.md`](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/docs/business-access-rules.md:291) says Bums may create captures and represented contacts only for destinations they are allowed to access. Live Supabase inventory on 2026-06-06 still shows this path is active enough to matter: `opportunity_registrations` 97, `customer_targets` 82, `extension_page_captures` 1, and `bum_contacts` 2.
- Why it matters: A Bum can currently discover unrelated accepted opportunities from the contact-link workflow and attach relationship evidence outside approved work. That weakens access boundaries, pollutes relationship data, and makes any later client-visible contact conversion harder to trust.
- Recommendation: Scope destination options and server-side mutation validation to Bum-entitled opportunities and targets only. Treat represented-contact linking as a privilege derived from an accepted claim, assigned target workflow, or other explicitly approved Bum relationship, not from broad accepted-opportunity visibility.
- Acceptance criteria: Bum contact create/update/detail flows return only entitled destinations; unrelated destination IDs are rejected server-side; QA proves one allowed and one denied cross-company or unrelated-destination case; client-visible workflow objects never derive from a raw contact linked to an unauthorized destination.

### P0 - Split finance-safe exports from operational exports
- Evidence: [`/client/exports`](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/src/App.tsx:151) is still available to `CLIENT_ADMIN` and `CLIENT_FINANCE`. [`ClientExports`](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/src/pages/client/ClientExports.tsx:77) exports target contact names and emails, while [`ClientExports`](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/src/pages/client/ClientExports.tsx:94) exports meeting attendees, `teams_join_url`, and `transcript_sync_status`. [`docs/business-access-rules.md`](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/docs/business-access-rules.md:330) explicitly says Client Finance should not export target contact emails, attendee lists, Teams join URLs, transcripts, or other operational workflow detail unless a finance-case exception is defined.
- Why it matters: The current export route gives finance users a shipped path to operational contact and meeting context that the business rule does not allow. That is both an access-control mismatch and a support-policy mismatch.
- Recommendation: Break `/client/exports` into finance-safe and operational bundles. Keep payment, commission, invoice, and finance-report exports available to `CLIENT_FINANCE`; move target contacts, attendees, join links, transcript fields, and other operational context behind `CLIENT_ADMIN` or a future documented exception rule.
- Acceptance criteria: `CLIENT_FINANCE` can export finance-safe payment data only; finance users cannot export target contacts, attendees, Teams join URLs, transcript fields, or other operational workflow context by default; QA covers one allowed finance export and one denied operational export.

### P1 - Turn admin access-request review into a proof-based workspace
- Evidence: [`AdminClients`](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/src/pages/admin/AdminClients.tsx:457) renders a `Company Access Review` card with direct approve/deny buttons only. - Acceptance criteria: Admins can filter pending requests by type, age, and company/domain; each request shows the resulting company/domain/role mutation before approval; public-email and related-domain approvals require a reviewer note or proof category; audit events retain request type, actor, company/domain context, and reason.

[`AdminPayments`](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/src/pages/admin/AdminPayments.tsx:107) filters reports by source and invoiced state only, and [`AdminPayments`](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/src/pages/admin/AdminPayments.tsx:176) only advances invoices to `SENT` or `PAID`. - Why it matters: Finance failures come from exceptions, not from the happy path. Without explicit queues for uninvoiced, sent-not-paid, paid-not-allocated, disputed, or variance-review states, the team will discover issues late and reconcile them manually.

## Workflow Map

- Profile bootstrap and access: signed-in user declares Client or Bum intent -> domain and email proof determines auto-approval, same-domain request, public-email company request, related-domain review, or Bum signup review -> Client Admin or Admin approves the allowed request type -> approval changes `profiles.access_status`, `role`, `company_id`, `client_access_role`, or approved-domain records.
- Public intake and handoffs: visitor submits contact form -> anti-abuse checks and notification attempt run -> `contact_submissions` enters the admin handoff queue -> Admin claims, escalates, converts, replies, or closes the intake.
- Opportunity and relationship work: client, Bum, or admin activity creates Opportunity-like work -> claims, target responses, intro requests, conversations, meetings, and transcripts attach -> accepted work later drives payment, invoice, and payout operations.
- Finance flow: customer payment is recorded -> invoice is generated, sent, and paid -> payout allocation should follow or enter an exception queue.
- Capture and represented-contact flow: Bum capture creates raw relationship evidence -> raw capture and represented-contact linking must stay limited to Bum-entitled destinations -> any future client-visible output should come from an approved converted projection, not raw capture rows.
