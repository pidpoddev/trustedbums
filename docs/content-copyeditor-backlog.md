# Trusted Bums Content Copyeditor Backlog

_Last updated: 2026-05-31 by Codex daily content copyeditor automation._

## Executive Read

The strongest current content risks are still taxonomy problems in trust-sensitive workflows, not sentence-level polish. Current source and live QA evidence both show three implementation-ready issues: the client legal journey still switches between `Agreements`, `Partner Terms`, `Terms & Legal Agreements`, `Client Agreement`, and `agreement records`; the client marketplace queue still collapses multiple objects into the broad word `requests`; and client team management still uses bare `Admin`, `Finance`, and `Member` labels even though the access model is explicitly client-scoped.

Current external guidance still supports the same direction. Digital.gov says users should understand content the first time and warns against repeating the same concept with different words. GSA says content should use clear headings, intentional calls to action, and consistent language. W3C guidance says visible labels and instructions should clearly communicate purpose, and identical functions should be identified consistently across pages ([Digital.gov introduction to plain language](https://digital.gov/resources/an-introduction-to-plain-language), [Digital.gov short and simple](https://digital.gov/guides/plain-language/principles/short-simple), [GSA content standards](https://www.gsa.gov/reference/gsa-web-style-guide/content-standards), [W3C labels or instructions](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/labels-or-instructions.html), [W3C consistent identification](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG20/Understanding/consistent-identification)).

## Company Glossary

### Client Agreement
- Definition: The current client contract a client user reviews and accepts to continue using the platform.
- Use when: Client legal acceptance pages, company profile legal cards, dashboard reminders, and public legal references to the client-specific agreement.
- Avoid/conflicts: `Partner Terms`, `Terms & Legal Agreements`, `Agreements` when the UI means the current contract rather than the broader legal center or history.
- Evidence: [src/pages/client/ClientTerms.tsx](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/src/pages/client/ClientTerms.tsx), [src/pages/client/ClientAgreements.tsx](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/src/pages/client/ClientAgreements.tsx), [src/data/legalDocuments.ts](/Users/ryan.peterson/Documents/Trusted%20Bums%20LOCAL/trustedbums/src/data/legalDocuments.ts)

## Disambiguation Queue

### Prospect client vs client vs customer
- Confusion risk: The Bum customer-lead flow still uses `prospect client` while the broader product also distinguishes paying `clients` from end-buying `customers`.
- Affected audiences: Bums, client admins, support, sales, analytics.
- Recommended direction: Validate whether `prospect client` is a business-critical marketplace term. If it remains, pair it with helper copy that contrasts the client-side company with the end customer account.
- Evidence needed: Sales collateral, CRM stage names, onboarding materials, and support language that define the prospect-to-client marketplace motion.

### Prospective Bum vs become a Bum vs Bum invite
- Confusion risk: The public intake says `I want to become a Bum`, while admin follow-up uses `Create Prospective Bum`, `Prospective Bum created`, and `Mark Bum Invited`, which reads like multiple stages without explicit stage language.
- Affected audiences: Public prospects, admin operators, support, recruiting, legal.
- Recommended direction: Choose one noun family for the intake object and separate stage labels for public interest, internal qualification, and invitation status.
- Evidence needed: Recruiting or partner-acquisition copy, onboarding emails, support macros, and any legal-approved description of how someone becomes a Bum.
